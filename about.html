About Kitoko Hearth

Our Story

A home, built around a hearth, for young people who want to build with technology.

How we started

It began with a question

A small group of young people kept asking the same question: "Why does learning to code feel so far from actually building anything?" So a handful of mentors and organizers built a different kind of program — one where every session ends with something real, and every student is treated like a builder from day one.

What started as informal weekend meetups around borrowed laptops grew into Kitoko Hearth: a structured, project-based program with real curriculum, real mentors, and a real community behind it. The name says it plainly — "kitoko" means beautiful, and a hearth is where people gather to stay warm, share stories, and build a life together. That's what we wanted school to feel like.

"Learning shouldn't feel like waiting. It should feel like making."

🎯

Mission

To give young people practical, hands-on technology education that leads directly to real, shareable skills — regardless of their background or starting point.

🔭

Vision

A world where any curious young person, anywhere, has a warm and welcoming path into a career in technology — built on real projects, not gatekeeping.

What we stand for

Core Values

Build first

We believe understanding follows from making. Every concept is anchored to something students actually build.

Everyone belongs

No prior experience, equipment, or background should stand between a curious person and a seat at the table.

Warmth over pressure

We hold high standards with a soft touch — mistakes are part of the process, not a reason to feel behind.

Community endures

Cohorts end. The relationships, mentorship, and support built inside Kitoko Hearth are meant to last well beyond them.
